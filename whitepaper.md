## What Is Axie World Cup?

Axie World Cup is an open-source community experiment built independently of Sky Mavis.

Participants gift an Axie and receive a random World Cup Country NFT representing one of the 48 nations qualified for the FIFA World Cup.

The collection is minted exclusively during the tournament and combines:

* Axie collecting
* World Cup engagement
* On-chain ownership
* Community rewards

The project is intentionally simple:

**Gift Axies. Collect Countries. Follow the World Cup. Earn Rewards.**

---

# Why Does It Exist?

Axie World Cup is an experiment.

The goal is to explore whether community-led initiatives can create measurable value for the Axie ecosystem without introducing new tokens or inflationary rewards.

The project introduces a voluntary **Axie sink**.

Every Country NFT minted requires one Axie to be released.

As participation increases:

* More Axies are removed from circulation.
* More Country NFTs enter circulation.
* Collection competition increases.
* Reward competition increases.

The project also aims to establish a precedent for future community-created events inside the Axie ecosystem.

If successful, the framework can be reused for future tournaments, seasonal events, and community experiments.

# How It Works

### Mint Window

The collection is only mintable during the World Cup.

**Opens:** 11 June 2026 — 19:00 GMT

**Closes:** 19 July 2026 — 19:00 GMT

After the mint window closes, no additional Country NFTs can be created.

The final supply is determined entirely by community participation.

### Participation

1. Select an Axie from App.Axie.
2. Transfer it to the Axie World Cup contract. (0x9F0ba160473aB48027CB1B6C0fc166cc66F9F9FB)
3. The contract forwards the Axie to the release wallet. (0x27263b825BaB7Ef905718185342905FBd248D1Ed)
4. A random Country NFT is assigned.
5. The NFT is minted directly to the participant.

Each gifted Axie generates exactly one Country NFT.

### Collection

* 48 Countries
* ERC-1155 collection
* Unlimited duplicates
* On-chain minting
* Tradeable on supported marketplaces

# Rewards

Total Reward Pool: **150 AXS** (Self-funded)

### Winning Country — 50 AXS

Shared between holders of the country that wins the World Cup.

### Top Scoring Country — 25 AXS

Shared between holders of the country that scores the most goals.

### Knockout Stage Countries — 25 AXS

Shared between holders of countries that reach the Round of 32.

### Full Collection Bonus — 50 AXS

Shared between wallets holding all 48 Country NFTs.

# Economics

Axie World Cup is designed as a non-profit community experiment.

The objective is not revenue generation but measuring whether community participation can create more ecosystem value than the rewards distributed.

### Success Metric

The primary metric is:

> Released Axies × Axie Floor Price > Reward Pool Value

At current prices:

* Axie Floor ≈ $0.80
* AXS ≈ $1
* Reward Pool = 150 AXS

Under these assumptions, approximately **188 released Axies** would be considered a successful outcome.

Additional indicators include:

* Number of participants
* Number of countries collected
* Number of completed collections
* Marketplace activity
* Community engagement throughout the tournament

### Royalties

No Royalties yet.

A future royalty rate between **5% and 10%** may be introduced if marketplace support becomes available.

If implemented:

* 80% would fund future rewards and operations.
* 20% would be allocated to the project treasury.

Any change would be announced publicly before activation.

# Technical Design

The project was intentionally designed to minimize trust requirements.

Many community projects require users to trust operators to hold assets, distribute rewards, or manage randomness.

Axie World Cup minimizes these concerns through on-chain automation.

### No Wallet Connection Required

Participants do not need to connect a wallet to the website.

Users can interact directly through App.Axie by transferring an Axie to the collection contract.

### On-Chain Minting

Country NFTs are minted directly by the contract.

There is no manual distribution process.

### Random Assignment

Country assignment uses Ronin VRF to provide transparent and verifiable randomness.

### VRF Costs

All randomness costs are paid by the project.

Participants do not pay additional fees beyond their transfer transaction.

### Transparency

All critical actions occur on-chain:

* Axie transfers
* Country assignment
* NFT minting
* Ownership verification

The website acts primarily as an informational interface.

The collection, ownership records, and reward eligibility remain independently verifiable through the Ronin blockchain even if the frontend disappears.

# Conclusion

Axie World Cup is a community-driven Axie sink experiment designed to test whether collecting, global events, and on-chain ownership can create measurable value for the Axie ecosystem.

The project is intentionally simple, transparent, and non-profit.

Its success will not be measured by revenue, but by participation, engagement, and the number of Axies transformed into a new collectible experience.

# Personal Vision

The World Cup collection is just the first step.

The real goal is to prove that community-led initiatives can create measurable value for the Axie ecosystem.

Axie already has the talent. Developers, designers, artists, and operators are willing to build. What is missing is a framework that turns ideas into experiments.

I would like to see recurring community hackathons around specific themes. Builders get a few days to ship. Projects are presented to the community every two or three months.

The objective is not to fund ideas. The objective is to fund results.

Projects compete on measurable outcomes, engagement, and ecosystem value created. The strongest projects receive AXS from the treasury to fund reward pools and continue development. Others remain free to launch independently and prove themselves in the market.

This creates a constant pipeline of experiments and gives governance real data instead of opinions.

A simple principle should guide treasury spending:

> The ecosystem should create more value than it spends.

Axie World Cup focuses on one theme: an Axie sink.

The next experiment might focus on Mystic utility, collectibles, AXS utility, SLP utility, RON utility, or another ecosystem challenge worth solving.

What matters is creating a culture where builders ship, measure results, share what they learn, and continuously add value to the ecosystem !
